# A5 Post-Closure Reopening Gate

## Purpose

This addendum freezes the conservative interpretation of A5 single-hive
anticipatory predictive control after the seed `7..16` closure and residual-gap
diagnostic. It does not supersede the original preregistration in
`docs/a5_anticipatory_predictive_control_preregistration.md`; it records what
must be true before A5 can be reopened for new mechanics, broader seeds, or
renewed structured-dynamics claims.

No simulator mechanics, new runs, dashboards, real LLM calls, Lean, Slack,
browser automation, Atomspace integrations, live task boards, or multi-hive
coupling are authorized by this document.

## Frozen A5 Interpretation

A5 established a useful deterministic scaffold: resource-bounded predictors can
improve forecast skill under matched single-hive demand streams while preserving
the existing OmegaSim artifact discipline. That result is narrow. The current
evidence does not support residual lobe grammar, strange-attractor-like
collective dynamics, or promotion beyond a closed single-hive predictive-control
pilot.

The scientific interpretation remains:

- prediction effort is a scarce managed resource, not a free analytic overlay;
- low and medium prediction budgets can improve forecast skill in the current
  scaffold;
- the observed forecast-skill gains did not survive the preregistered
  full-accounting residual-structure gate;
- budget-matched timing-broken nulls removed the promotion-relevant residual
  interpretation;
- oracle prediction remains a smoothing positive control, not a target dynamic.

## Required Reopening Conditions

Any future A5 reopening must begin with a new preregistration before
implementation. The preregistration must define, at minimum:

- the scientific claim being tested and why it differs from the closed seed
  `7..16` result;
- the deterministic task-arrival stream, service capacity, action opportunity,
  work budget, and seed matching rules;
- the prediction-budget accounting, including whether prediction spend directly
  competes with work, coordination, cleanup, or research opportunity;
- reactive, linear or short-memory, bounded nonlinear, oracle, and
  budget-matched shuffled or phase-randomized predictor conditions;
- primary residual endpoints, lead-lag endpoints, forecast-skill-per-budget
  endpoints, and guardrails for backlog, queued age, completion fraction,
  starvation, and volatility;
- surrogate-null and accounting-control rules sufficient to fail closed;
- the exact promotion and closure criteria before any new seed run.

Changing prediction from a recorded condition field into a resource that
competes directly with work opportunity is a scientifically different design. It
requires explicit work-budget accounting and cannot be introduced as a minor
scaffold extension.

## Allowed A5 Work Without Reopening

The following remain allowed without reopening A5:

- read-only audits of existing A5 artifacts;
- documentation that clarifies the closed interpretation;
- tests or analyzer checks that demonstrate a concrete bug in already tracked
  A5 logic;
- mechanical maintenance that preserves existing A5 outputs and conclusions.

The following are not allowed without a new preregistration:

- broader A5 seed sweeps;
- new predictor mechanics or richer lobe labels;
- multi-hive anticipatory coupling;
- target, phase, or transfer-null experiments;
- external integrations or live OmegaHive task sources;
- promotion language based on secondary return-map or lobe-like hints.

## Downstream Boundary

Three-hive delayed anticipatory coupling remains downstream of A5. If it is ever
requested, it should be treated as a separate stage with target/phase nulls,
matched transfer opportunity, and resource-bounded cross-hive prediction costs.
It should not inherit promotion from the closed single-hive A5 forecast-skill
result.
