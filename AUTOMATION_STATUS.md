The scheduler-ablation pilot-vs-holdout synthesis is complete. The demand-vs-service-capacity holdout on seeds `70..99` supports the load-accounting interpretation while preserving a residual trajectory signal in point estimates: higher service capacity reduces load-normalized backlog at fixed pressure and increases lobe-transition entropy, while higher creation pressure increases load-normalized backlog at every fixed service level and reduces transition entropy. The paired bootstrap control supports the signs of nearly all trajectory/load effects, but the label-count-preserving null control makes the independent lobe-locking interpretation more conservative because observed-minus-null entropy/dwell differences are small. The decision synthesis includes load/action accounting, confirming that task creation pressure changes created-task counts and action mix while service capacity changes task-work opportunity. The queue-blind lobe robustness pass, which excludes queue depth, queue delta, and default baseline lobe labels, still shows pressure structure: extreme-minus-normal pressure increases task-generation dwell share by roughly `0.47..0.49`, lowers transition entropy most strongly at low/baseline service, and high service capacity shifts dwell share toward execution. The primary frozen-rate exogenous-arrival holdout on seeds `70..99` now decouples demand from agent create-task pressure: exogenous rate increases monotonically raise total created tasks, final load-normalized backlog, and queued age while agent-created task totals remain near the endogenous control. Queue-blind entropy falls modestly at the high exogenous rate, but queue-blind task-generation dwell share does not grow. Treat this as evidence for load pressure plus a residual trajectory point estimate, not yet independent emergent lobe grammar until paired bootstrap and null controls are added for the exogenous holdout.
